🌿 Aeroponic Farming

What is Aeroponic Farming?

Aeroponic farming is a modern technique where plants grow without soil. Their roots are suspended in air and sprayed with nutrient-rich mist. It is widely used in smart farming and urban agriculture.

How It Works

Roots hang freely and receive nutrients through mist spraying systems.

  • No soil required
  • Fast growth
  • Efficient nutrient use

Technology Used

Uses sensors, pumps, timers, and automated systems to control plant growth.

Advantages 🌟

  • Uses 90% less water
  • No soil diseases
  • High yield
  • Space saving

Disadvantages ⚠️

  • High setup cost
  • Technical knowledge required
  • Power/system failure risk

Government supports modern farming like aeroponics through subsidies and training programs.

Supports irrigation and water-saving technologies.

MIDH

Provides subsidies for greenhouse & aeroponics systems.

Future of Aeroponics 🚀

Aeroponics is the future of farming, especially in urban areas and space research. It helps solve food shortages and supports sustainable agriculture.
